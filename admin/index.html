    About Us

    The Tiny Miracles Foundation started with picking up one child from the street to get her educated. However, soon they realized the parents did not understand why their child should go to school in the first place. Seeing this happen often and listening to their needs led to a realization that in order to break the cycle of poverty, a mindset shift needs to take place. People need hope and tools for a better future. Through trial and error, the nonprofit has designed a holistic approach that focuses on all areas of life, and not just educating children.

    Our Result

    Women Empowerment

    There are no longer any child marriages in the communities.The communities have now accepted girls marrying outside their caste and outside of the community, where before they would have been outcasts.Widows are allowed to remarry, which was never allowed before.All women know how to use the bank & many can save money every month. Before, almost none women had bank accounts and no savings.All women now have accident insurance under the government insurance scheme. Many women obtain life insurance.We see a clear trend towards women investing for the future: buying small apartments outside the city and gold.

    Education

    All children capable go to English schools, and all those less capable go to Hindi/Marathi schools.All children go to after-school homework classes.All girls now aim for higher education, whereas before none of the girls would because their parents wouldn’t allow it.The first children have completed their graduation programs, supported by Tiny Miracles. In 2016, the first girl even obtained a Graduate degree for the first time in the history of the Pardeshi community. The majority (18-45 years) of women are literate now; many were illiterate before.


    Healthcare

    All children in the communities are immunised and are in stable health, whereas at the start, the majority were malnourished and dealing with frequent malaria and tuberculosis. After years of structural doctor visits and health sessions in the community, most inhabitants are now very aware of how to stay healthy and/or receive proper treatment for (chronic) diseases. All communities now have the right knowledge to independently visit doctors and support each other if needed.Community members now have enough resources to pay for visits without assistance from Tiny Miracles. Assistance is only needed for the most vulnerable categories, such as the elderly without any family to support them.



    Social awareness

    Over 100 awareness sessions are organised yearly, ranging from trips to the bank to healthy cooking classes to sessions on how to deal with sexual abuse to daughter-in-law and mother-in-law meetings. The communities are healthy, they know how to stay healthy and they now have health cards to get free medical assistance from the nearby hospital.Group bonding & friendships have developed among families that first did not talk to each other due to deeply rooted conflicts. Group formation enables community members to tackle challenges together. For example, to keep the community safe and clean and to take care of the vulnerable and elderly. Communities are now prioritizing getting the right medical assistance vs alternative approaches. This is crucial as they wouldn’t always get the right medical assistance before, even in cases of emergency.
