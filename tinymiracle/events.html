    Our 5 Pillars




    1. Social Awareness


    When life in poverty is all you know, every day tends to be a struggle, and priorities are more commonly organized based on survival. To overcome this and give people the support they need, we cover over 150 topics during awareness sessions, ranging from financial literacy and the confidence to speak up about domestic abuse to hygiene and human rights. An empowered mindset is a capable mindset, and that shift is what we rally behind.




    2. Healthcare


    It’s true what they say: health is wealth. When people are sick or malnourished, change is less inspiring and more daunting. That’s why we work on the ground to improve the community’s health through weekly doctor visits, dentist visits, blood tests, supplementary nutrition, organized exercise programs, and addiction counseling..




    3. Education


    Knowledge is power, and power is potential. So, we prioritise education by testing and placing children in suitable programs, offering classes to the illiterate parent population, teaching English, and providing computer access and computer classes. High school graduates can even get a university loan from us.





    4. Skill Training


    Getting out of poverty takes time and money. That’s why we work to give illiterate, unskilled community members the chance to earn an income and open a bank account. We start by teaching sewing, folding, product manufacturing, and the basics of quality control. We make sure that these skills are useful and relevant for future endeavors. Earning money gives them the opportunity to make their own choices.
